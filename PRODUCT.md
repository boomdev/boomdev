# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences share this surface equally.

**Curious visitors** want a quick, honest map of what exists under the Boomdev umbrella: live products, experiments, research notes, and content projects. They are browsing, not buying a single SKU.

**Potential collaborators** are founders and builders working on similar problems. They use the site to understand the themes connecting the work and to reach out when a project or direction resonates.

Typical context: scanning on a laptop or phone between tasks, comparing projects, following links to individual sites. Low patience for corporate positioning or hype.

## Product Purpose

Boomdev is the public home base for a small group of independent software initiatives. It is an umbrella, not a single company with one product line.

Success looks like:

- Visitors understand what Boomdev is in under a minute.
- Projects are easy to discover, distinguish by status, and open on their own domains.
- The site feels credible and human, without inventing scale or authority the work does not have.
- Contact is low-friction for relevant conversations (Tally form when configured).

## Brand Personality

**Warm, approachable, builder-led.**

Voice is direct and plain. Favor clarity over cleverness. Sound like a small lab explaining its work to peers, not a brand campaign or investor deck.

Emotional goals: trust, orientation, quiet confidence. Visitors should feel they can see the whole picture without being sold to.

## Anti-references

Do not design or write toward:

- **VC-backed SaaS homepage** — purple gradients, hero metrics, interchangeable product UI marketing.
- **Fake agency or corporate site** — pretending to be a large company, generic “we innovate” language, stock authority cues.
- **AI hype page** — vague innovation claims, slop aesthetics (gradient text, glass cards, template card grids), “magic” framing.
- **SEO link farm** — keyword stuffing, backlink blocks, directory pages that exist only for search engines.

Avoid exaggerated startup language, fake scale, and claims not supported by the actual projects.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over spectacle** — Help people navigate real work; decoration must earn its place.
2. **Honest scope** — Present varied project statuses (product, experiment, research) without smoothing everything into one brand fantasy.
3. **Builder-to-builder tone** — Write for people who ship software; skip performative marketing.
4. **Practical craft** — Prefer useful layout, readable type, and accessible defaults over trend-chasing UI.
5. **Connective, not centralizing** — Boomdev links out to projects; it does not trap attention on itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target:** WCAG 2.2 **AA** as the baseline for all public content.
- **Stretch:** Apply **AAA** where it is straightforward (contrast on body text, focus visibility, meaningful motion reduction).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for decorative effects.
- External project links should be obvious to keyboard and screen reader users (new tab behavior communicated).
- Contact embed (Tally) must remain usable with keyboard and assistive tech when live.
